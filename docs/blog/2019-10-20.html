Ship's Log

A diary by Capt. Crit 'Red Beard' McGillicutty

By Crit McGillicutty
Island Breeze Tribune

Lost at sea

The sun’s fierce glare mocks me, high in the heavens, as our ship drifts aimlessly across this vast, empty stretch of water. The winds, once our trusty companions, have abandoned us, leaving only the slow creak of the wood beneath our feet. I stand at the helm, but the compass is little more than a trinket now—its needle spins in confusion, lost to the whims of the gods.

We are adrift, like a phantom ship, forgotten by the world and swallowed whole by the endless horizon. The crew's spirits grow thin, like a ragged sail torn by the storm's fury, though they speak little of their fear. There are no stars to guide us, no land in sight for days. I fear we’ve gone off course, but where to? We are marooned in a sea of uncertainty, trapped between the mercy of Poseidon and the wrath of the unknown.

The rationed stores are running low—hardtack and salted meat that no longer satisfy even the grumbling bellies of the crew. Water is scarce, and what we do have, I fear, may spoil. If the gods are kind, we'll find land soon. If not, we may see the black sails of our doom.

I cannot shake the feeling that we’ve angered something, some ancient spirit of the sea, and that our fate is now tied to the tides. The men speak in hushed whispers of strange shadows in the water—visions of creatures larger than ships, of eyes that gleam like lanterns beneath the waves. I do not know if these are madness or truth, but I know this: if we do not find land soon, I fear that our names will be lost to the sea, as if we were never more than fleeting whispers on the wind.
